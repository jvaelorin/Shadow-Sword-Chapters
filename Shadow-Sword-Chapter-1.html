A thread frays. A choice burns.

Chapter 1 — The Balance and the Unraveling

We were the guardians of peace.
What are we now?
It was simply mathematics.

His breath never shook. His voice never faltered.

Harsh logic draped in gentle words — that was Vaelorin's gift to them.

Where others hesitated, he acted. To him, the fading of the lesser Seers was not cruelty but an equation already solved.

"If we must sacrifice to end the Unraveling… let the sacrifice be swift."

In that breathless stillness, he named the truth the others feared to speak — and severed the first soul.

The sound was scarcely audible, a chime pulled loose from the world's edge. Above the Lumen Veil, Threads writhed, pulsing like veins through a wounded sky.

A flare echoed outward, final as a closing door.

Kessa fell like a cut Thread.

Her body struck stone with the finality of snapping light. Rivers of soul-fire spilled upward into the Veil's waiting embrace. She had been young once — hesitant, fragile, trusting. And because of that, she was perfect for the equation.

Seliane screamed.

She rushed forward, silver fire streaming from her robes, reaching for a sister already gone. Her grief was heavy, untempered — the very weight Vaelorin had carved out of himself.

"You didn't even look at her," she hissed. "You didn't even ask her name."

Vaelorin tilted his head — a gesture of polite consideration.

"Names fade," he said. "The Balance remains."

The Veil flared.

And for one hollow heartbeat, the trembling of the world ceased.

The Balance held.

Myrras looked away first. Not in shame, but in quiet thought, his flame still flaring, his tongue held. Etriin judged, but remained silent.

Seliane knelt beside the fading light. Her trembling hand pressed against Kessa's unraveling Threads, trying to gather what little remained.

"I remember... she couldn't stop crying her first night in the ward," her whisper caught. "She was so hesitant. So fragile. But she trusted me."

Her voice broke. Seliane lowered her cheek to Kessa's cooling skin.

"Shhh, little Thread... it's going to be alright. Big sister's here, I'll keep you taut through the night. I'll chase the storms, I'll— I'll…"

Her lips trembled. Her breath faltered against Kessa's brow. The words dissolved.

The air stilled.

Then, barely a whisper:

"She always asked me to sing it again. Just… one more time."

Her shoulders shook.

"I never said no."

She looked past Vaelorin — into the Veil's glow.

"She believed it every time."

A heartbeat of silence.

Then — a crack tore through the Threadspire, stone weeping as one of the high pillars split near its base.

Several heads turned as the Veil trembled.

A quiet sob escaped, but no one moved.

Vaelorin only lowered his hand, the afterimage of his word still pulsing in the air like a wound refusing to close.

He glanced to the fracture in the stone. Then to his palm — faintly glowing.

And said:

"Hmm. It appears my calculations were off—by one."
